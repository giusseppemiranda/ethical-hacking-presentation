# Speech Transcription — CEFR A2 (Elementary)

> Simple connected sentences, past tense introduced, basic connectors (and, but, because).

---

## Slide 1: The Breach (Lock being hacked)

"What you see here is a hacker trying to break into a system. They try many passwords — one after another. Most of them fail. But then... one works. They are inside now. This is how hacking starts."

---

## Slide 2: The Money (Scale balance)

"Look at this balance. On one side, a company pays a hacker 150,000 dollars. On the other side, a data breach costs 4.4 million dollars. The hacker's side is light. The breach side is very heavy. Companies save 30 times more money when they hire a hacker. It's the smartest decision."

---

## Slide 3: The Pentagon (USA fortress breached)

"In 2016, the US government did something unusual. They invited 1,410 hackers to attack the Pentagon. The first person found a problem in only 13 minutes. At the end, they found 138 security problems. The government was happy because now they could fix them."

---

## Slide 4: How They Work (Live terminal)

"Watch the screen. This is a real hacking terminal. First, the hacker scans the target — they find open ports. Then they look for weak points. Next, they exploit the vulnerability — they get inside. Finally, they write a report and send it to the company. They break in to help, not to steal."

---

## Slide 5: What Students Learn (Skill tree)

"What do students learn in this career? It's like a skill tree in a video game. You start with the basics: Linux, Python, and networking. Then you unlock harder skills: cryptography, SQL injection, firewalls, WiFi hacking. At the top: malware analysis, penetration testing, and red team operations. Each skill builds on the one before."

---

## Slide 6: Choose Your Path

"Now look at these two paths. On the left: criminal hacking. Prison, criminal record, no internet, destroyed future. On the right: ethical hacking. Good salary, work anywhere, bug bounties, growing career. The skills are the same. The choice is different. One path destroys your life. The other one builds it."

---

## Presentation Tips

- Speak at a moderate pace
- Use connectors: "but," "because," "then"
- Point at visuals when mentioning numbers
- Make eye contact during questions
- Use rising intonation to build curiosity
