# Speech Transcription — CEFR B1 (Intermediate)

> Connected discourse, opinions, conditionals, wider range of tenses and vocabulary.

---

## Slide 1: The Breach (Lock being hacked)

"Imagine you're watching someone break into a system in real time. Passwords are being tested — hundreds per second. Most fail. The screen flashes red again and again. But then, one attempt succeeds. Access granted. This is what ethical hackers do every day — except they do it legally, and they get paid for it."

---

## Slide 2: The Money (Scale balance)

"Watch this number climb — it's how much ethical hackers can earn. Now look at the scale. On one side: 150,000 dollars — what a company pays a hacker. On the other side: 4.45 million dollars — the average cost of a data breach. The balance tips dramatically. For every dollar spent on an ethical hacker, companies save about thirty. It's not charity — it's the smartest investment in cybersecurity."

---

## Slide 3: The Pentagon (USA fortress breached)

"In 2016, the United States Department of Defense launched a program called 'Hack the Pentagon.' They invited regular people — not soldiers, not spies — to hack their own systems. Over 1,400 hackers accepted the challenge. The first vulnerability was discovered in just 13 minutes. By the end, 138 security flaws had been found. The cost? Only 150,000 dollars. A traditional audit would have cost over a million."

---

## Slide 4: How They Work (Live terminal)

"What you're seeing now is a real hacking session. Watch the commands. First, the hacker runs a port scan — nmap — to discover what's running on the target. They find open ports, vulnerable services. Then they use specialized tools to probe deeper — and there it is: a SQL injection vulnerability. They exploit it, prove they can access the database. But here's the key moment: instead of stealing data, they generate a report. Fourteen vulnerabilities documented. Sent to the client. That's the ethical difference."

---

## Slide 5: What Students Learn (Skill tree)

"This is what the learning path looks like — a skill tree, just like in a video game. You start at the bottom with fundamentals: Linux, Python programming, and networking. These are your foundation. Then you unlock intermediate skills: cryptography, SQL injection, firewall evasion, WiFi penetration testing. And at the top? Advanced specializations: malware reverse engineering, full penetration testing, and red team operations. Each skill builds on the previous ones. The field is growing at 33 percent, with 3.5 million positions unfilled worldwide."

---

## Slide 6: Choose Your Path

"And now we arrive at the real question. Two paths, same skills. On the left: criminal hacking. Five years in prison. A criminal record that follows you forever. No internet access. A destroyed future. On the right: ethical hacking. Salaries up to 220,000 dollars. Work from anywhere in the world. Bug bounties. A career growing at 33 percent. The skills are identical. The intention makes all the difference. Three and a half million positions are waiting. Which side will you choose?"

---

## Presentation Tips

- Vary your pace — slow down for key numbers, speed up for action
- Use rhetorical questions to engage the audience
- Gesture toward the screen during visual transitions
- Maintain confident posture and project your voice
- Pause after revealing surprising facts for dramatic effect
